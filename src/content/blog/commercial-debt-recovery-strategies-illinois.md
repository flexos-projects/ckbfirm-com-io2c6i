For many businesses in Illinois, a delinquent account is not just a financial annoyance; it is a threat to operational cash flow and long-term stability. While internal accounts receivable teams do their best, some debtors require a more sophisticated, legal-centric approach to produce results. 

In our experience handling complex commercial collections, we have found that success is rarely about aggressive phone calls. Instead, it is about strategic leverage and technical legal precision.

## 1. Immediate Post-Default Investigation
The moment a commercial account enters default, the clock starts ticking. In Illinois, debtors often move assets or prioritize other creditors who apply the most effective pressure. We advocate for an immediate deep-dive into the debtor's current status. This includes checking for recently filed UCC liens, pending litigation in Cook County or federal courts, and property transfers. Knowing exactly who else is standing in line allows us to position your claim for maximum priority.

## 2. Leveraging Illinois Citation Proceedings
Once a judgment is obtained, the real work begins. Illinois law provides powerful tools through Supplementary Proceedings (735 ILCS 5/2-1402). By serving a Citation to Discover Assets, we effectively "freeze" the debtor's ability to transfer property. This is a critical tactical advantage. It forces the debtor to provide testimony and documentation regarding their financial health, often uncovering bank accounts or receivables that can be garnished immediately.

## 3. The Power of Third-Party Discovery
Often, the debtor is not the best source of information about their own assets. We frequently look to third partiesâ€”banks, vendors, and clients of the debtor. Under Illinois law, we can compel these entities to disclose and turn over assets belonging to the judgment debtor. This strategy is particularly effective in [commercial collection](/practice-areas/commercial-collections) scenarios where the debtor may be attempting to hide behind shell companies or complex corporate structures.

## 4. Strategic Settlement via Agreed Orders
Litigation is a tool, but recovery is the goal. Sometimes, a structured settlement is the most pragmatic path to being paid in full. However, we never settle on a handshake. We utilize "Agreed Orders" that allow for immediate entry of judgment or aggressive enforcement remedies if a single payment is missed. This keeps the pressure on the debtor and ensures that our clients remain the top priority in the debtor's monthly budget.
